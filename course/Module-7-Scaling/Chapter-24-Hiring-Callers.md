# Chapter 24: Hiring and Managing Callers

## Building Your Outreach Team

Callers are the front line of your business. They make first contact, qualify leads, and set up opportunities for you to close. This chapter covers how to hire, train, and manage an effective calling team.

---

## Types of Callers

### Cold Callers / Lead Qualifiers

**Role:** Make initial contact, qualify interest, set appointments

**Skills needed:**

- Clear communication
- Resilience (handles rejection)
- Follows scripts
- Good listener
- Basic objection handling

**Best for:** High-volume initial outreach

### Appointment Setters

**Role:** Qualify leads and schedule calls with closer

**Skills needed:**

- All cold caller skills plus:
- Better qualification ability
- Calendar management
- Warm handoff skills

**Best for:** Warm transfer model

### Closers / Sales Representatives

**Role:** Convert qualified leads into signed clients

**Skills needed:**

- Advanced communication
- Objection handling mastery
- Relationship building
- Closing ability
- Product knowledge

**Best for:** High-value conversions

---

## The Calling Models

### Model 1: You Close Everything

```
Caller qualifies → Transfers to you → You close
```

**Pros:**

- You control the close
- Higher conversion rate
- Learn what works

**Cons:**

- Limited by your time
- Must be available for transfers

**Best for:** Starting out, high-value leads

### Model 2: Appointment Setting

```
Caller qualifies → Schedules callback → You call and close
```

**Pros:**

- More flexibility
- Can batch closing calls
- Caller doesn't need to transfer live

**Cons:**

- Some leads cool off
- No-shows happen
- Longer sales cycle

**Best for:** When you can't take live transfers

### Model 3: Full Sales Team

```
Caller qualifies → Closer closes → You oversee
```

**Pros:**

- Fully scalable
- You're not in the sales process
- Can handle high volume

**Cons:**

- Higher cost
- Quality control challenges
- Must hire good closers

**Best for:** Mature operations, maximum scale

## Hiring Callers

### Where to Find Callers

**Offshore (Philippines, Latin America):**

- OnlineJobs.ph
- Upwork
- VA agencies
- Cost: $5-12/hour

**US-Based:**

- Indeed
- ZipRecruiter
- Craigslist
- Cost: $12-25/hour + commission

**Specialized:**

- Call center agencies
- Real estate calling services
- Cost: Varies widely

### Job Posting for Callers

```
Title: Outbound Caller - Surplus Funds Recovery

We're looking for motivated callers to help homeowners
recover money they're owed.

What You'll Do:
- Make outbound calls to property owners
- Follow proven scripts
- Qualify interest and gather information
- Set appointments or transfer to closers
- Document all calls in CRM

Requirements:
- Excellent English communication
- Comfortable on the phone
- Resilient - can handle rejection
- Reliable internet and quiet workspace
- Available [hours/schedule]

Experience Preferred:
- Previous cold calling experience
- Real estate or financial services background
- CRM experience

Compensation:
- Base: $[X]/hour
- Bonus: $[X] per qualified appointment/transfer
- Performance bonuses available

To Apply:
Send a 1-minute voice recording introducing yourself
and explaining why you'd be great at this role.
```

### Screening Callers

**Voice Recording Test:**

- Require voice sample in application
- Evaluate: clarity, energy, professionalism
- Eliminate poor communicators immediately

**Phone Interview:**

- Call them unexpectedly
- See how they answer
- Conduct brief interview
- Evaluate phone presence

**Role Play Test:**

- Give them a simple script
- Have them "call" you
- Evaluate delivery and adaptability
- See how they handle pushback

**Trial Period:**

- Start with paid trial (1-2 weeks)
- Monitor calls closely
- Evaluate metrics
- Make permanent hire decision

## Training Callers

### Training Curriculum

**Day 1: Foundation**

- Company overview
- What surplus funds are
- Why we help people
- Compliance and ethics

**Day 2: Scripts and Process**

- Opening scripts
- Qualification questions
- Common responses
- CRM documentation

**Day 3: Objection Handling**

- Top 10 objections
- Response frameworks
- Practice sessions
- Role playing

**Day 4: Systems and Tools**

- Dialer training
- CRM training
- Call documentation
- Transfer procedures

**Day 5: Supervised Calling**

- Make calls with supervision
- Real-time feedback
- Adjust and improve
- Build confidence

**Week 2: Monitored Independence**

- Independent calling
- Daily call reviews
- Ongoing coaching
- Performance tracking

### The Caller Script Package

Provide callers with:

1. **Opening script** - First 30 seconds
2. **Qualification script** - Questions to ask
3. **Objection responses** - Top 20 objections
4. **Closing/transfer script** - How to hand off
5. **Voicemail script** - What to leave
6. **Follow-up scripts** - Second, third attempts

### Role Play Scenarios

Practice these situations:

1. Interested and ready
2. Skeptical but listening
3. "It's a scam"
4. "Not interested"
5. "Call me back later"
6. Angry/hostile
7. Confused/doesn't understand
8. Wants to do it themselves

## Managing Caller Performance

### Key Metrics to Track

| Metric             | Definition                | Target |
| ------------------ | ------------------------- | ------ |
| Calls per hour     | Dials made                | 15-25  |
| Contact rate       | Answers / Dials           | 20-30% |
| Conversation rate  | Conversations / Contacts  | 50-70% |
| Qualification rate | Qualified / Conversations | 20-30% |
| Transfer/Appt rate | Transfers / Qualified     | 50-70% |
| Show rate          | Shows / Appointments      | 70-80% |
| Close rate         | Closes / Shows            | 20-40% |

### Call Monitoring

**Listen to calls regularly:**

- New callers: Daily
- Experienced callers: Weekly
- Random sampling
- Focus on problem areas

**What to evaluate:**

- Script adherence
- Tone and energy
- Objection handling
- Professionalism
- Accuracy of information

**Feedback framework:**

1. Start with positives
2. Identify improvement areas
3. Provide specific examples
4. Offer solutions
5. Set expectations
6. Follow up

## Compensation Structures

### Base + Commission Model

**Example:**

- Base: $10/hour
- Per qualified transfer: $10
- Per signed deal: $50-100

**Pros:**

- Motivates performance
- Aligns incentives
- Rewards results

**Cons:**

- Can encourage cutting corners
- More complex to track
- May create competition issues

### Hourly + Bonus Model

**Example:**

- Hourly: $12/hour
- Weekly bonus for hitting targets: $100
- Monthly bonus for top performer: $200

**Pros:**

- Simpler to manage
- Team-oriented
- Predictable costs

**Cons:**

- Less individual motivation
- May not reward top performers enough

### Performance Tiers

**Example:**
| Performance Level | Hourly Rate |
|-------------------|-------------|
| Training (Week 1-2) | $10 |
| Standard | $12 |
| Above Target | $14 |
| Top Performer | $16 |

## Common Caller Problems and Solutions

### Problem: Low Contact Rate

**Possible causes:**

- Bad data (wrong numbers)
- Poor timing
- Caller giving up too fast

**Solutions:**

- Improve skip tracing quality
- Test different calling times
- Require minimum ring time
- Verify data before calling

### Problem: Low Conversion Rate

**Possible causes:**

- Poor script delivery
- Weak objection handling
- Wrong tone/energy
- Not building rapport

**Solutions:**

- More role playing
- Call monitoring and feedback
- Script adjustments
- Additional training

### Problem: High Turnover

**Possible causes:**

- Low pay
- Burnout
- Poor management
- Unrealistic expectations

**Solutions:**

- Competitive compensation
- Reasonable call volumes
- Regular breaks
- Recognition and appreciation
- Clear growth path

### Problem: Compliance Issues

**Possible causes:**

- Inadequate training
- Pressure to perform
- Unclear guidelines

**Solutions:**

- Comprehensive compliance training
- Clear do's and don'ts
- Regular monitoring
- Zero tolerance for violations

## Scaling Your Calling Team

### When to Add Callers

Add a caller when:

- Current callers are at capacity
- You have more leads than can be called
- You're turning away opportunities
- Quality is maintained

### Team Structure at Scale

**5-10 Callers:**

- You manage directly
- Daily team huddles
- Weekly 1-on-1s

**10-20 Callers:**

- Add team lead
- Team lead manages day-to-day
- You manage team lead

**20+ Callers:**

- Multiple team leads
- Call center manager
- You oversee managers

- Compensate fairly with performance incentives
- Address problems quickly
- Scale thoughtfully
