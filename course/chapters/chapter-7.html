Foreclosure is one of the most traumatic financial experiences a person can go through. Understanding this is crucial to your approach.

Stages of Foreclosure Grief

  1. Denial: "This can't be happening to me"
  2. Anger: "The bank/system did this to me"
  3. Bargaining: "Maybe I can still save it"
  4. Depression: "I've lost everything"
  5. Acceptance: "I need to move forward"

The FORD Method

Build connection by discussing:

  • Family - "How's the family settling into the new place?"
  • Occupation - "What do you do for work?"
  • Recreation - "Getting back to normal routines?"
  • Dreams - "What are you looking forward to?"

Active Listening Techniques

  • Let them talk without interrupting
  • Acknowledge their feelings
  • Reflect back what you hear
  • Ask follow-up questions
