# Chapter 20: Hiring Virtual Assistants

## Finding and Hiring the Right People

Virtual assistants can transform your business, but only if you hire the right people. This chapter covers where to find VAs, how to evaluate them, and how to set up successful working relationships.

---

## Types of Virtual Assistants

### General Virtual Assistants

**Best for:**

- Administrative tasks
- Data entry
- Basic research
- Email management
- Scheduling

**Typical cost:** $5-15/hour (offshore), $15-30/hour (US-based)

### Specialized Virtual Assistants

**Research VAs:**

- Skip tracing
- Property research
- Lead generation
- Data mining

**Cold Calling VAs:**

- Outbound calls
- Lead qualification
- Appointment setting
- Follow-up calls

**Administrative VAs:**

- Document preparation
- Claims filing
- Client communication
- Bookkeeping

**Typical cost:** $8-25/hour depending on specialization and location

## Where to Find Virtual Assistants

**Upwork (upwork.com)**

- Largest freelance marketplace
- Wide range of skills and prices
- Built-in time tracking and payments
- Reviews and ratings system

- Quick turnaround

## The Hiring Process

### Step 1: Define the Role

Before posting, clarify:

- Specific tasks they'll perform
- Hours needed (part-time/full-time)
- Schedule requirements (time zones)
- Skills required
- Tools they'll use

**Example Role Definition:**

```
Position: Research Virtual Assistant
Hours: 20 hours/week
Schedule: Flexible, some US business hours overlap
Tasks:
- Research foreclosure records in assigned counties
- Calculate surplus fund amounts
- Perform skip tracing using provided tools
- Enter data into CRM
- Organize and prioritize lead lists

Required Skills:
- Excellent English (written)
- Attention to detail
- Experience with data entry
- Comfortable with online research
- Reliable internet connection

Tools Provided:
- CRM access
- Skip tracing service login
- Training materials
```

### Step 2: Create Job Posting

**Effective Job Post Elements:**

1. Clear title
2. Company/business description
3. Specific responsibilities
4. Required qualifications
5. Preferred qualifications
6. Hours and schedule
7. Compensation range
8. How to apply

**Sample Job Post:**

```
Title: Virtual Assistant for Surplus Funds Research

We're a growing surplus funds recovery business looking
for a detail-oriented virtual assistant to help with
research and data management.

About Us:
We help former homeowners recover surplus funds from
foreclosure sales. We're a small, growing company with
a positive, supportive culture.

Responsibilities:
- Research foreclosure records and surplus funds
- Perform skip tracing to locate property owners
- Enter and organize data in our CRM
- Prepare lead lists for outreach
- Basic administrative tasks as needed

Requirements:
- Excellent written English
- Strong attention to detail
- Experience with data entry and research
- Reliable internet and computer
- Available 20 hours/week with some US hours overlap

Preferred:
- Experience in real estate or legal research
- Familiarity with CRM systems
- Previous VA experience

Compensation: $X-Y/hour based on experience

To Apply:
Please send:
1. Brief introduction about yourself
2. Relevant experience
3. Your availability
4. Answer: What does "attention to detail" mean to you?
```

### Step 3: Screen Applications

**Red Flags:**

- Generic, copy-paste responses
- Didn't answer screening question
- Poor English/communication
- Unrealistic availability claims
- No relevant experience

**Green Flags:**

- Personalized response
- Answered all questions
- Clear communication
- Relevant experience
- Professional presentation

### Step 4: Interview Top Candidates

**Interview Questions:**

_Experience:_

- "Tell me about your previous VA experience."
- "What types of research have you done before?"
- "Describe a time you had to learn a new system quickly."

_Skills:_

- "How do you stay organized when managing multiple tasks?"
- "What do you do when you're stuck on a task?"
- "How do you ensure accuracy in your work?"

_Fit:_

- "Why are you interested in this role?"
- "What are your long-term career goals?"
- "How do you handle feedback and corrections?"

_Practical:_

- "What's your internet speed and backup plan?"
- "What hours are you available?"
- "What tools/software are you comfortable with?"

### Step 5: Test Before Hiring

**Paid Test Project:**

- Give a small, real task
- Pay for their time (1-3 hours)
- Evaluate quality and communication
- See how they handle instructions

**Example Test:**

```
"I'd like you to research 10 properties from this list.
For each property, find:
1. The foreclosure sale date
2. The sale price
3. The owner's name at time of foreclosure

Enter the information in this spreadsheet.
Time estimate: 2 hours
Pay: $[X] for completed test

This will help us both see if this is a good fit."
```

### Step 6: Make the Hire

**Offer should include:**

- Hourly rate or salary
- Expected hours
- Start date
- Trial period (30 days recommended)
- How payment works
- Next steps for onboarding

## Setting Up for Success

### Before They Start

**Prepare:**

- [ ] Training materials/videos
- [ ] Written SOPs for each task
- [ ] Tool access and logins
- [ ] Communication channels
- [ ] First week's tasks

### First Day/Week

**Onboarding checklist:**

- [ ] Welcome call/video
- [ ] Overview of business
- [ ] Review of their role
- [ ] Tool setup and training
- [ ] First tasks assigned
- [ ] Check-in schedule established

### Ongoing Management

**Daily:**

- Brief check-in (5-10 min)
- Answer questions
- Review completed work

**Weekly:**

- Longer check-in (30 min)
- Review performance
- Provide feedback
- Plan next week

**Monthly:**

- Performance review
- Discuss improvements
- Adjust responsibilities
- Compensation review (if applicable)

## Common Hiring Mistakes

### Mistake 1: Hiring Too Fast

**Problem:** Didn't properly vet candidates
**Solution:** Always interview and test

### Mistake 2: Unclear Expectations

**Problem:** VA doesn't know what success looks like
**Solution:** Define tasks, metrics, and standards clearly

### Mistake 3: No Training

**Problem:** VA struggles without guidance
**Solution:** Create thorough training materials

### Mistake 4: Micromanaging

**Problem:** Wastes your time, frustrates VA
**Solution:** Set expectations, then trust and verify

### Mistake 5: No Feedback

**Problem:** VA doesn't improve
**Solution:** Regular, constructive feedback

## Chapter Summary

- Define the role clearly before hiring
- Use appropriate platforms for your needs
- Screen carefully and always test before hiring
- Pay fair rates for quality work
- Set up proper onboarding and training
- Establish clear communication systems
- Provide regular feedback and support
