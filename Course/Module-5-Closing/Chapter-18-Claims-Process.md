# Chapter 18: The Claims Process

## From Signed Contract to Recovered Funds

Once you have a signed client, the real work begins. This chapter walks you through the claims process from start to finish, including common challenges and how to overcome them.

---

## Overview of the Claims Process

### Typical Timeline

```
Week 1-2: Document Gathering
    ↓
Week 2-3: Claim Preparation
    ↓
Week 3-4: Claim Filing
    ↓
Week 4-12: Processing Period
    ↓
Week 12-16: Approval and Disbursement
```

**Total: 2-4 months typical (varies by jurisdiction)**

---

## Phase 1: Document Gathering

### Documents You Need to Obtain

**From the Client:**

- [ ] Copy of government-issued ID
- [ ] Proof of current address
- [ ] Social Security Number (for tax purposes)
- [ ] Signature on claim documents

**From Public Records:**

- [ ] Deed showing client as owner at foreclosure
- [ ] Foreclosure sale documentation
- [ ] Surplus funds verification
- [ ] Any lien information

**From the County/Court:**

- [ ] Official claim form
- [ ] List of required documentation
- [ ] Filing instructions
- [ ] Fee schedule (if any)

### How to Obtain Documents

**Property Records:**

- County recorder's office
- Online property databases
- Title companies

**Court Records:**

- Clerk of court
- Online court portals
- PACER (federal cases)

**Surplus Verification:**

- County treasurer
- Clerk of court
- Trustee who conducted sale

## Phase 2: Claim Preparation

### Step-by-Step Preparation

**Step 1: Verify Eligibility**

- Confirm client was owner at time of foreclosure
- Check for competing claims
- Verify no liens that would take priority
- Confirm statute of limitations hasn't expired

**Step 2: Complete Claim Form**

- Obtain correct form for jurisdiction
- Fill out completely and accurately
- Double-check all information
- Have client review before signing

**Step 3: Compile Supporting Documents**

- Organize in required order
- Make copies of everything
- Create cover letter/index
- Ensure all signatures are original or properly notarized

**Step 4: Client Review and Signature**

- Send documents for review
- Explain what they're signing
- Obtain signatures (electronic or physical)
- Verify signatures match ID

### Common Claim Form Requirements

| Field               | What to Include                      |
| ------------------- | ------------------------------------ |
| Claimant name       | Exact legal name as on deed          |
| Property address    | Full address of foreclosed property  |
| Sale date           | Date of foreclosure sale             |
| Claim amount        | Surplus amount being claimed         |
| Basis for claim     | Former owner, heir, lienholder, etc. |
| Contact information | Current address and phone            |

## Phase 3: Filing the Claim

### Filing Methods

**In Person:**

- Bring original and copies
- Get stamped receipt
- Note case/claim number
- Ask about timeline

**By Mail:**

- Send certified mail with return receipt
- Keep copies of everything
- Include self-addressed stamped envelope
- Follow up to confirm receipt

**Electronic (if available):**

- Follow portal instructions
- Save confirmation
- Print/save all receipts
- Note submission number

### Filing Checklist

- [ ] Claim form completed and signed
- [ ] All required supporting documents
- [ ] Filing fee (if required)
- [ ] Cover letter
- [ ] Copy for your records
- [ ] Proof of filing method

## Phase 4: Processing Period

### What Happens During Processing

1. **Initial Review**: Clerk checks for completeness
2. **Verification**: County verifies claim details
3. **Notice Period**: Other potential claimants notified
4. **Objection Period**: Time for challenges
5. **Final Review**: Approval or denial decision
6. **Disbursement Order**: Authorization to pay

### Following Up

**When to follow up:**

- 2 weeks after filing: Confirm receipt
- 30 days: Check status
- 60 days: Check status again
- Ongoing: Every 2-4 weeks until resolved

**How to follow up:**

```
"Hi, I'm calling to check on the status of a surplus
funds claim. The case number is [number], filed on
[date] for property at [address]. Can you tell me
the current status?"
```

**What to ask:**

- Has the claim been assigned to someone?
- Is anything missing or needed?
- What's the expected timeline?
- Are there any issues I should know about?

### Keeping Clients Informed

**Update schedule:**

- After filing: Confirm filed, provide case number
- Every 2-4 weeks: Status update
- When status changes: Immediate notification
- Upon approval: Celebration and next steps

**Sample update email:**

```
Subject: Update on Your Surplus Funds Claim

Hi [Name],

I wanted to update you on your surplus funds claim.

Status: [Current status]
Filed: [Date]
Case Number: [Number]

[Brief explanation of where we are in the process]

Expected next step: [What happens next]
Estimated timeline: [When to expect resolution]

I'll continue following up and will let you know as
soon as there's any news.

Questions? Feel free to call me at [number].

Best,
[Your Name]
```

## Phase 5: Approval and Disbursement

### When Claim is Approved

1. **Receive approval notification**
2. **Verify disbursement details**
3. **Confirm payment method and timing**
4. **Notify client immediately**
5. **Coordinate fee payment**

### Disbursement Methods

**Check to client:**

- Most common method
- Client receives full amount
- Client pays your fee separately

**Check to you (if authorized):**

- Some jurisdictions allow
- You deduct fee and forward balance
- Requires specific authorization

**Split checks:**

- Separate checks to client and you
- Requires court order usually
- Cleanest for fee collection

### Collecting Your Fee

**Option 1: Client pays from proceeds**

```
"Congratulations! Your claim has been approved. You'll
receive a check for $[amount] within [timeframe].

As per our agreement, my fee of $[fee] is due upon
receipt. You can pay by [payment methods].

Thank you for trusting me with your claim!"
```

**Option 2: Direct from disbursement**

- Requires proper authorization
- Cleaner for both parties
- May require court approval

**Option 3: Assignment**

- Fee assigned from proceeds
- Check issued to you for fee amount
- Check your state's laws on assignments

## Handling Complications

### Common Issues and Solutions

**Issue: Missing Documents**

```
Solution: Contact county for specific requirements,
obtain missing documents, refile if necessary.
```

**Issue: Competing Claims**

```
Solution: Research other claimants, determine priority,
may need legal counsel if disputed.
```

**Issue: Claim Denied**

```
Solution: Understand reason for denial, correct issues
if possible, appeal if appropriate.
```

**Issue: Client Unresponsive**

```
Solution: Multiple contact attempts, document efforts,
may need to pause claim.
```

**Issue: Heir Complications**

```
Solution: May need probate, identify all heirs,
possibly refer to attorney.
```

### When to Involve an Attorney

- Claim is denied and you want to appeal
- Competing claims create legal dispute
- Complex heir/probate situations
- High-value claims with complications
- Client wants to sue for additional damages

## Jurisdiction-Specific Processes

### Judicial vs. Non-Judicial Foreclosure States

**Judicial Foreclosure States:**

- Claims filed with the court
- May require court hearing
- Judge approves disbursement
- More formal process

**Non-Judicial Foreclosure States:**

- Claims filed with trustee or county
- Administrative process
- Less formal
- Often faster

### Research Your Target Jurisdictions

For each county you work in, document:

- [ ] Where to file claims
- [ ] Required forms
- [ ] Required documentation

- [ ] Typical timeline
- [ ] Contact information

## Tracking Your Claims

### CRM/Tracking System

Track for each claim:

- Client information
- Property details
- Surplus amount
- Filing date
- Case/claim number
- Current status
- Next action needed
- Follow-up dates
- All communications

### Status Categories

1. **Research**: Gathering information
2. **Preparation**: Preparing claim documents
3. **Ready to File**: Documents complete
4. **Filed**: Submitted, awaiting processing
5. **In Review**: Being processed
6. **Additional Info Needed**: County requested more
7. **Approved**: Claim approved
8. **Disbursement Pending**: Waiting for payment
9. **Completed**: Funds received, fee collected
10. **Denied/Closed**: Claim unsuccessful
