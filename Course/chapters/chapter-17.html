üìã Essential Documents

1. Service Agreement / Retainer üìù

The primary contract between you and the client.

Must include:

  • Your company information
  • Client information
  • Property address and surplus amount
  • Services you will provide
  • Fee percentage and when it's due
  • What happens if unsuccessful
  • Cancellation rights
  • Signatures and date

2. Authorization to Act üîê

Gives you permission to act on their behalf.

Must include:

  • Client's full legal name
  • Property address
  • Specific authorization language
  • Scope of authority
  • Duration of authorization
  • Notarization (often required)

3. Assignment of Proceeds (if applicable) üí∞

Directs payment of your fee from the proceeds.

4. Information Release üì§

  • Permits access to court records

üìú Sample Contract Provisions

Fee Clause Example

COMPENSATION: Client agrees to pay Company a 
contingency fee equal to thirty percent (30%) of 
all surplus funds recovered. This fee is due and 
payable upon successful recovery of funds. If no 
funds are recovered, Client owes nothing.

Scope of Services Example

SERVICES: Company agrees to:
a) Research and verify surplus funds availability
b) Prepare and file all necessary claim documents
c) Communicate with relevant government agencies
d) Follow up on claim status
e) Notify Client of claim resolution

Cancellation Clause Example

CANCELLATION: Client may cancel this agreement 
within three (3) business days of signing without 
penalty. After this period, Client may cancel but 
agrees to pay Company for work performed at a rate 
of $150 per hour, not to exceed the contingency 
fee amount.
