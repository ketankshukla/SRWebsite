üëÄ Recognizing Buying Signals

Watch for these signs they're ready to move forward:

  • Asking about the process details
  • Asking about timeline
  • Asking what they need to provide
  • Tone becomes more relaxed
  • They start using "when" instead of "if"
  • They mention what they'll do with the money

üéØ Closing Techniques

1. The Assumptive Close

Assume they're moving forward and proceed accordingly.

"Great! Let me get your information so we can get 
started. What's the best email address to send the 
paperwork to?"

2. The Summary Close

Summarize the benefits and ask for commitment.

"So to recap: there's approximately $[Amount] waiting 
for you. I handle all the paperwork and filing. You 
pay nothing unless I'm successful, and you'll receive 
your funds in about [Timeframe].

Ready to get started?"

3. The Alternative Close

Give them a choice between two positive options.

"Would you prefer to sign the paperwork electronically 
today, or would you rather I mail you a physical copy?"

4. The Direct Close

Simply ask for the business.

"Based on everything we've discussed, are you ready 
to move forward with recovering your funds?"

5. The Urgency Close

Use legitimate time factors.

"The escheatment deadline for your funds is [Date]. 
To make sure we have plenty of time, I'd recommend 
we get started this week. Can we do that?"

6. The Trial Close

Test their readiness before the final ask.

"How does everything sound so far? Do you have any 
other questions before we proceed?"
