Documents You Need to Claim Surplus Funds

By Ketan Shukla November 12, 2025 7 min read

Filing a surplus funds claim requires proper documentation. Without the right paperwork, your claim can be delayed or denied — even if you're legitimately entitled to the money. This comprehensive checklist will help you gather everything you need before filing.

Essential Documents Checklist

While requirements vary by state and county, most surplus fund claims require some combination of the following documents:

Identity Verification

Government-Issued Photo ID

A valid driver's license, state ID, or passport. The name must match the name on the property records. If your name has changed (marriage, divorce), you'll need documentation of the name change.

Social Security Card or Number

Some jurisdictions require your SSN for tax reporting purposes, as surplus funds may be considered taxable income.

Proof of Ownership

Copy of the Original Deed

The deed showing you as the property owner at the time of foreclosure. This can be obtained from the county recorder's office if you don't have a copy.

Title Insurance Policy

If you have it, your title insurance policy from when you purchased the property can serve as additional proof of ownership.

Closing Documents

The HUD-1 settlement statement or closing disclosure from your original purchase can help establish ownership.

Foreclosure Documentation

Foreclosure Sale Confirmation

Documentation showing the sale occurred, the sale price, and the date. Available from the county clerk or court that handled the foreclosure.

Notice of Default or Foreclosure Notice

Any notices you received about the foreclosure proceedings. These help establish the timeline and your connection to the property.

Heir or Estate Claims (If Applicable)

Death Certificate

If the original owner is deceased and you're claiming as an heir, you'll need a certified copy of the death certificate.

Letters Testamentary or Letters of Administration

Court documents appointing you as executor or administrator of the estate.

Probate Court Order

If the estate went through probate, documentation showing the distribution of assets.

Affidavit of Heirship

A sworn statement establishing your relationship to the deceased owner, often required when there's no will.

Where to Obtain Missing Documents

County Recorder's Office

  • Copies of deeds
  • Property transfer records
  • Lien information
  • Foreclosure sale records

County Clerk or Court

  • Foreclosure case files
  • Sale confirmation documents
  • Surplus fund deposit records

Probate Court

  • Letters testamentary
  • Estate distribution orders
  • Heir determinations

Vital Records Office

  • Birth certificates
  • Death certificates
  • Marriage certificates
  • Name change documents

Pro Tip: Request certified copies of all documents. Many jurisdictions won't accept regular photocopies — they require documents with official seals or stamps.

Special Situations

Name Changes

If your name has changed since you owned the property, you'll need:

  • Marriage certificate (if name changed through marriage)
  • Divorce decree (if name changed through divorce)
  • Court order (if name changed through legal petition)

Multiple Owners

If the property had multiple owners:

  • All owners may need to sign the claim
  • Or you'll need power of attorney from other owners
  • Or documentation showing you're the sole remaining owner

Business or Trust Ownership

If the property was owned by an LLC, corporation, or trust:

  • Articles of incorporation or organization
  • Operating agreement or bylaws
  • Resolution authorizing the claim
  • Trust documents showing beneficiaries

Tips for a Successful Claim

  1. Start gathering documents early: Some documents take weeks to obtain
  2. Make multiple copies: Keep originals safe and submit copies unless certified originals are required
  3. Organize chronologically: Present documents in logical order
  4. Include a cover letter: Summarize your claim and list all enclosed documents
  5. Follow up: After submitting, confirm receipt and ask about processing time
