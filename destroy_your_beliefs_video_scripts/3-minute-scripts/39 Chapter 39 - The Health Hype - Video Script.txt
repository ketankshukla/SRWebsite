Superfoods. Detox cleanses. The latest diet. That supplement everyone's talking about.

Health and wellness is a multi-billion dollar industry. And much of it is built on hype, not science.

Let's cut through the noise.

Diet trends come and go. Low-fat was the answer. Then low-carb. Then paleo. Then keto. Then intermittent fasting. Each one promised to be the key to health.

The reality? Nutrition science is complicated and often uncertain. Most diet studies have serious limitations. The dramatic claims rarely match the evidence. And the best diet is probably... boring. Mostly plants, reasonable portions, not too processed.

Supplements are mostly unnecessary. Unless you have a specific deficiency, most vitamins and supplements don't helpâ€”and some may harm. The industry thrives on the belief that you can optimize health through pills. You mostly can't.

"Natural" doesn't mean safe or effective. Many alternative therapies lack scientific support. Some delay treatment for serious conditions. The appeal of "natural" often overrides critical thinking.

Even mainstream medicine isn't perfect. Recommendations change as research evolves. Conflicts of interest exist. The human body is complex and not fully understood.

So how do you navigate this?

Be skeptical of dramatic claims. If something sounds too good to be true, it probably is. Real health improvements are usually gradual and boring.

Look for scientific consensus, not single studies. One study doesn't prove anything. Look for what the overall body of evidence shows.

Watch for red flags. Miracle cures. Secret knowledge. Testimonials instead of data. Attacks on mainstream medicine. Products sold by the person giving advice.

Focus on fundamentals. The basics are well-established and unsexy: sleep, movement, nutrition, stress management, social connection. These matter more than any supplement or superfood.

Accept uncertainty. We don't have all the answers about health. Anyone claiming certainty is probably selling something.

Consult real professionals. For serious health concerns, see qualified healthcare providers, not influencers.

Your body isn't a machine to be optimized with the right hacks. It's a complex system that mostly needs basics done consistently.
